Rabo Encendido

Description

Rabo Encendido is a rich and flavorful Cuban oxtail stew, slow-cooked in a savory tomato-based sauce infused with garlic, bell peppers, onions, and traditional spices. The name “encendido” (meaning "on fire") refers to its slight spiciness, a rarity in Cuban cuisine. This dish is known for its deep, hearty flavor and fall-off-the-bone tenderness, making it a favorite for special occasions and family gatherings.

Originally influenced by Spanish and African culinary traditions, Rabo Encendido became a staple in Cuban kitchens due to its ability to transform an inexpensive cut of meat into a luxurious, comforting meal. It's typically served with white rice and fried plantains to soak up the rich sauce.

Ingredients

For the Oxtail:

  • 3 lbs oxtail, trimmed of excess fat
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil

For the Sauce:

  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup red wine (optional but recommended)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon sugar (to balance acidity)
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 large carrot, chopped
  • 1 large potato, chopped
  • ½ cup green olives (optional)

For Serving

  • White rice
  • Fried plantains

How to Make Cuban Rabo Encendido

  1. Prepare the Oxtail:
    1. Rinse the oxtail under cold water and pat dry with paper towels.
    2. Season with salt, black pepper, cumin, paprika, and oregano.
    3. In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium-high heat.
    4. Brown the oxtail on all sides, then remove from the pot and set aside.
  2. Build the Flavorful Sauce:
    1. In the same pot, sauté the onions, bell peppers, and garlic until softened.
    2. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1 or 2 minutes to enhance the flavor.
    3. Add crushed tomatoes, red wine, and beef broth, stirring to combine.
    4. Mix in bay leaf, red pepper flakes, sugar, Worcestershire sauce, and carrots.
  3. Simmer the Stew:
    1. Return the browned oxtail to the pot, ensuring it's submerged in the sauce.
    2. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 2.5 to 3 hours, stirring occasionally.
    3. After 2 hours, add the chopped potatoes and olives (if using).
    4. Continue cooking until the meat is fall-off-the-bone tender and the sauce thickens.
  4. Serve and Enjoy:
    1. Remove the bay leaf before serving.
    2. Serve hot over white rice with a side of fried plantains.
