Vaca Fita

Description

Vaca Frita, which translates to “fried cow,” is a popular Cuban dish featuring tender, marinated beef that's pan-fried until crispy on the edges while remaining juicy inside. The dish is infused with garlic, lime, and onions, giving it a bright and zesty flavor. Unlike Ropa Vieja, which is stewed, Vaca Frita is dry-fried to achieve its signature crispiness.

This dish has Spanish and Caribbean influences, with the frying technique adding a unique Cuban twist. Often served with white rice, black beans, and fried plantains, Vaca Frita is a simple yet incredibly flavorful meal that highlights the bold, fresh flavors of Cuban cuisine.

Ingredients

For the Beef:

  • 2 lbs flank steak or skirt steak
  • 4 cups water (for boiling)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced

For the Marinade:

  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons sour orange juice (or substitute with equal parts orange and lime juice)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

For Frying:

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)

For Serving

  • White rice
  • Black beans
  • Fried plantains

How to Make Cuban Vaca Frita

  1. Cook the Beef:
    1. Place the flank steak in a large pot with water, salt, black pepper, oregano, cumin, and garlic.
    2. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 1.5 hours, or until the meat is tender.
    3. Remove the meat from the broth, allow it to cool slightly, and then shred it into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Marinate the Shredded Beef:
    1. In a bowl, combine lime juice, sour orange juice, and 1 tablespoon olive oil.
    2. Toss the shredded beef in the marinade and let it rest for at least 15 minutes.
  3. Fry the Beef for Crispiness:
    1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    2. Add the sliced onions and sauté until softened.
    3. Push the onions to the side and add the shredded beef in an even layer.
    4. Let the beef fry undisturbed for 3 to 4 minutes, allowing it to crisp up.
    5. Stir and flip occasionally until golden brown and crispy on the edges.
  4. Serve and Enjoy:
    1. Remove from heat and serve hot with white rice, black beans, and fried plantains.
    2. Squeeze extra lime juice on top for an extra burst of flavor.
